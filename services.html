Applications

With over 20 years experience filing trademarks, we are able to provide you with and efficient and effective service to protect your trademarks in Brazil.

Application

Trademarks in Brazil are filed with each class as a separate application. Once the application is filed, it takes about 8 weeks for publication by the Brazilian Trademark Office that the application is open for opposition by third parties. Currently about 14 months to be informed that the application has been accepted. Once all fees have been paid, it takes a few weeks for the certificate to be available, in pdf only.

Documents

Required documentation to file a trademark in Brazil comprises of a Power of Attorney and an Affidavit (a declaration of business activity). These can be signed and returned via email without the need for legalization. Additional documents are required to transfer ownership, and require legalisation.

Trademark management

Each week, we search for publications related to your applications. These can be to advise you that your application has been received and open for oppposition, oppositions, demands from the Brazilian Trademark Office and that your trademark as been granted. 

Monitoring

Each week, we search the most recent new applications for trademarks that might be similar to yours. If we find something that might be considered an infringement, you can decide what to do next.

Activity in Brazil

The number of new trademarks varies each week, but often, there are more than 10,000 trademarks published. Most of course are Brazilian companies but there is a growing number filed by foreign companies. Most are with the Brazilian Trademark Office but a reasonable number via WIPO.
